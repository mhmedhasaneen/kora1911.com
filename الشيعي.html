They will take on the Baltimore Ravens at 1 p.m. ET on Sunday at M&T Bank Stadium. The 49ers made easy work of the Green Bay Packers last week and carried off a 37-8 victory. San Francisco's success was spearheaded by the efforts of TE George Kittle, who caught six passes for 129 yards and one TD, and QB Jimmy Garoppolo, who passed for 253 yards and two TDs on 20 attempts. Garoppolo ended up with a passer rating of 155.80. As for Baltimore, Baltimore can't say they have nothing to be thankful for this Thanksgiving week. They put a hurting on the Los Angeles Rams to the tune of 45-6. QB Lamar Jackson had a dynamite game for Baltimore; he picked up 95 yards on the ground on eight carries and threw five passing touchdowns. Jackson ended up with a passer rating of 139.40. Their wins bumped San Francisco to 10-1 and Baltimore to 9-2. A pair of stats to keep in the back of your head while watching: The 49ers rank first in the league when it comes to yards allowed per game, with only 248 on average.
